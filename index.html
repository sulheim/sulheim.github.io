Systems biologist
bridging computation
and experiment

I use metabolic modeling, multi-omics, and wet-lab experiments to understand how microbial metabolism shapes ecosystem function — and to guide microbiome design. My research spans three complementary phases: genome-scale metabolic modeling in Eivind Almaas' lab at NTNU and the Segrè Lab at Boston University; applied biotech, bioinformatics, and data management at SINTEF Industry; and microbial ecology, microbiome research, and wet-lab work in Sara Mitri's group at the University of Lausanne, as part of the Swiss NCCR Microbiomes. I have contributed to ~20 grant applications, secured competitive personal funding twice, and delivered results both in peer-reviewed journals and to industrial collaborators.

I have a strong analytical mindset and a love for challenges — whether that means building a new computational pipeline, designing an experiment from scratch, or bridging the two. Hard-working and solution-oriented, I thrive at the interface of academia, research institutes, and industry.

Expertise

Computational and experimental

A rare combination in systems biology: I build the models and run the experiments to test them.

Genome-scale metabolic modeling

Construction, curation and simulation of GEMs for bacteria and microbial communities. Applied to strain engineering, natural product biosynthesis, and community-level metabolism in Streptomyces, Prochlorococcus, and gut microbiome contexts.

  • GEM reconstruction
  • Flux balance analysis
  • Enzyme-constrained models

Experimental microbiology

Hands-on wet-lab work at SINTEF Industry and the University of Lausanne — microbial culturing, metabolomics, and experimental validation of computational predictions. Experienced in teaching undergraduate microbiology laboratory courses.

  • Microbial culturing
  • Metabolomics
  • Omics data integration
  • Lab course teaching

Bioinformatics & software tools

Building open-source computational tools for biological research: automated biosynthetic gene cluster analysis, metabolic pathway reconstruction, and omics integration pipelines. Strong emphasis on reproducibility and usability.

Microbial community research

Studying metabolite exchange, competition, and cooperation in microbial communities — from natural ecosystems to engineered co-cultures. Current work at UNIL focuses on metabolite release dynamics and the ecological economics of microbial communities.

Do microbes actively regulate which metabolites they release into the environment, and if so, by what mechanism?

Integrated metabolomics, genomics, and transporter analysis across bacterial strains to show that metabolite release rate correlates with metabolite value, mediated by active transport.

npj Biofilms and Microbiomes 10, 17

Question

How do different methanogenic archaea affect the electrochemical activity, stability, and community structure of Geobacter spp. biofilm anodes in combined anaerobic digestion and microbial electrochemical systems?

Approach

Exposed pre-grown Geobacter biofilm anodes to three model methanogens and characterized the resulting changes using electrochemical measurements, 16S rRNA metabarcoding, whole metagenome nanopore sequencing, and shotgun sequencing.
